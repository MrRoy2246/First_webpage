Tenet is a 2020 science fiction action thriller film written and directed by Christopher Nolan, who also produced it with his wife Emma Thomas. A co-production between the United Kingdom and the United States, it stars John David Washington, Robert Pattinson, Elizabeth Debicki, Dimple Kapadia, Michael Caine, and Kenneth Branagh.

Story Line

A secret agent is given a single word as his weapon and sent to prevent the onset of World War III. He must travel through time and bend the laws of nature in order to be successful in his mission.

Details

The film follows a former CIA agent who is recruited into a secret organization, tasked with tracing the origin of objects that are traveling backwards through time and their connection to an attack from the future to the present.

Box Office

As of January 29, 2024, Tenet has grossed $58.5 million in the United States and Canada and $306.8 million in other territories, for a worldwide total of $363.7 million.[2][1] With a production budget of $200 million,[116] it is Nolan's most expensive original project.[117] IndieWire speculated that marketing costs pushed the final sum to $300–350 million,[118] though some analysts predicted it would incur lower advertising costs than usual, owing to inexpensive live sports ads.[119] Box office analyst Jeff Bock estimated the film would need to make $400–500 million to break even.[120] In November 2020, rival studios expected the film to lose up to $100 million, but Warner Bros. insisted losses would not top $50 million.[121] Nolan reportedly received twenty percent of the film's first-dollar gross.[122]
